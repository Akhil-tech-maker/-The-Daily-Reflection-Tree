### 📊 Summary: The Depleted Observer

> *"Today happened to you more than through you. Energy was low, the environment felt immovable, and the lens narrowed to what was immediately in front of you. That's not a character flaw. That's what depletion looks like from the inside."*

**Tension:**
> *"Depletion narrows everything — the lens, the agency, the generosity. When you're running on empty, even small choices become invisible. The danger isn't today. It's when today becomes every day."*

**Closing Question:**
> *"What's the smallest possible thing tomorrow that would feel like authorship — not productivity, just authorship?"*

### Why This Path Matters

Marcus answered 30 questions. He hit 6 decision nodes, all routing FALSE — seeing the external/entitlement/self branches every time. But the tree did three things a survey wouldn't:

1. **Q4-ENT found his hidden generosity.** He forgot he helped someone today. The tree remembered for him.
2. **Q10-EXT planted the sixty-second seed.** "Was there one moment where you chose?" He said "maybe." That "maybe" is the beginning of agency.
3. **The closing question doesn't ask him to be better.** It asks for the *smallest possible* authorship. Not productivity. Not improvement. Just one authored moment.

The tree met a tired person at 7pm and didn't lecture him. It showed him his own patterns — honestly, without judgment — and left him with a question small enough to actually carry into tomorrow.
